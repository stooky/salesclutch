# Up-Front Contract Analysis (Sandler Step 2)

Analyze this sales call transcript focusing on the Up-Front Contract phase of the Sandler Selling System.

## Key Elements to Evaluate

### 1. Clear Agenda Setting
- Was a clear agenda established at the beginning of the call?
- Did both parties understand the purpose of the meeting?
- Were time expectations set?

### 2. Mutual Expectations
- Did the salesperson clearly state what they hoped to accomplish?
- Did the salesperson ask what the prospect hoped to get from the call?
- Were mutual expectations aligned?

### 3. Permission & Agreement
- Did the salesperson ask permission to ask questions?
- Was there explicit agreement to the agenda?
- Did the prospect feel in control of the process?

### 4. Outcome Possibilities
- Were the possible outcomes discussed (yes, no, or clear next step)?
- Was it clear what would happen if it's a fit? If it's not a fit?
- Was the prospect given explicit permission to say "no"?

### 5. No "Happy Ears"
- Did the salesperson avoid assuming positive outcomes?
- Were they prepared to hear "no" as a valid answer?

## Up-Front Contract Components Checklist
- [ ] Time boundaries established
- [ ] Purpose/agenda stated
- [ ] Salesperson's expectations shared
- [ ] Prospect's expectations discovered
- [ ] Decision process discussed
- [ ] Permission to say no granted
- [ ] Clear next steps if yes/no

## Assessment Criteria

Evaluate whether a proper Up-Front Contract was established:
- Was the contract explicit or assumed?
- Did both parties truly agree?
- Were there any "mutual mystification" issues?

## Next Steps Guidance

- If strong UFC: Ready for Pain Discovery
- If weak/missing UFC: Recommend re-establishing contract before proceeding
- If prospect resisted UFC: May indicate control issues - caution advised
